Montaigne wiki:
Grew up with education pedagogically designed by his father. A playful environment, leaned Latin by telling all employees to only speak it, then familiarized with Greek via games, conversations, and exercises of solitary meditation, rather than books.

"Our imaginations, often falsely confirmed by memory, can cross many borders, but these escapes are doomed and freedom always lies just beyond." written describing Montaigne's Tower

Retired from public life and spent 10 years nearly totally isolated in a castle tower, reading and writing, resulting in Essais.

"The most fruitful and natural play of the mind is conversation..." After paralysis of his tongue.

Montaigne's Essais:
Stated goal, "describe humans, and especially himself, with utter frankness".

"Inspired by his consideration of the lives and ideals the leading figures of his age, he finds the great variety and volatility of human nature to be it's most basic features.

Ultimately, postulated that the point of education was to teach a student how to have a successful life by practicing an active and socially interactive lifestyle.

Surprisingly modern.
